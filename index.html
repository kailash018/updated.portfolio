Getting strong in competitive programming

The art of making machines work as per your instructions is simply called programming. The advanced form of programming which deals with the real-world problems and where you have to compete with other programmers is called competitive programming. To survive the coming era of continuous advancements, programmers need to master their competitive coding skills.

When you are used to of writing code for resolving real-world problems, it is high time for you to focus on shortening your code. Remove unnecessary lines, eliminate repetitive code, and learn optimisation of the code. This is the most important and the transition phase from basic programming to competitive programming.
Once you have learned how to build an optimal solution to a real-life problem, you are ready to take part in the coding competitions like hackathons or code sprints. Here, you have to write the best optimal solution within the shortest time possible. Such platforms offer a healthy competitive environment that makes you learn at a faster rate and helps you assess your programming skills.

Ways to deal with programmers burnout !!

Burnout is a very common and serious issue in the IT industry. Developers feel stuck working very hard for long periods on projects but it seems impossible to complete. If somebody doesn’t take action at the right time then later it can become too dangerous for developers and they can quit programming as well. They may fall deep into it and struggle to get out of its hideous embrace. Burnout is like a tunnel that just keeps going so it’s better to avoid it before the situation becomes worse for you. We are going to discuss some tips to avoid it.

* Take regular breaks throughout the day. Sitting 8-10 hours in front of the computer without any break won’t help you in being productive.
* Try to do exercise at least 2-3 times a week or do swimming. Include healthy food, veggies, nuts, and fruits in your diet. Get enough sleep and use a noise machine.
